# Why I built FirstWeek

> The founder's story behind the product. Surfaced in-app at `/story`.
> This is a draft in Ammar's voice — edit freely; it's your story.

FirstWeek started the week I got laid off.

I never had the strongest formal education. What I had was curiosity and real
experience — years of actually doing the work, untangling messy problems, keeping
things running. But every time I found a job I knew I could do, I'd stop myself.
*No degree for this. Not "qualified" on paper.* I talked myself out of roles I
was genuinely capable of — especially the mid-level and senior ones.

Then it clicked: I'm not the only one. So many capable people are frozen in the
same place — able to do the job, held back by a credential and a quiet voice
asking, *"who are you to apply?"*

A resume can't prove you can do the work. Doing the work can. So FirstWeek turns
any job posting into the real tasks you'd face in that role — you try them, get
honest feedback, and find out, *before you apply*, that you can actually do this.

I've walked into interviews I wasn't "qualified" for on paper and done well —
because I could do the work. FirstWeek is the tool I wish I'd had the day I got
laid off. If you've ever second-guessed yourself in front of a job you know you
can do, this is for you. Go nail that interview.

— Ammar

## Why this matters for the product

The story is the positioning. FirstWeek isn't a resume tool — it's a confidence
tool for capable people without the "right" paper. Keep the copy honest and
non-hype (see `PRODUCT_SPEC.md` trust requirements); the emotional hook is real
precisely because it isn't exaggerated.
